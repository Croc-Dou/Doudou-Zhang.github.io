Data Scraping

Data Scraping

We collect two data sets from two websites. First, we collect a list of 5167 movie titles from "the-numbers" website. Then we save the titles into a csv file named data.csv. Next, we search those titles from IMDB website, and collect data from the web page.

After data collecting, we now have 27 variables for 5043 movies. Over all, they span across 100 years in 66 countries. There are 2399 unique director names, and 30K actors/actresses. Roughly speaking, half of the variables is directly related to movies themselves, such as title, duration, country, etc. Another half is related to the people who involved in the production of movies, for instance, director Facebook popularity, IMDB rating score, etc.

Prediction

1 Correlation analysis

First, we drop aspect_ratio and the link because we don't think these parameters could affect the rating of a movie.

Then we select all of the numeric features and do the correlation.

It seems that the actors facebook likes are highly correlated with the total which is normally considering it's the sum of the others, so we just remove it and make 2 categories : actor_1_facebook_likes and other_actors_facebook_likes.

More over there is a big correlation between the num_voted users and the number of reviews from both critics and users, the gross and the movie_facebook_likes. So we take the mean of those as a new variable called movie_success.

2 Random Forest

The variables we use to do the random forest analysis are movie_success, duration, director_facebook_likes, actor_1_facebook_likes, other_actors_facebook_likes and budget. And the mean validation sore we get from the random forest is 19.6%, which means that the percentage of variables explained is 19.6%

Limitations

We've noticed that the facebook likes data we collect might not reflect the movie's true Facebook likes since the IMDB Facebook likes only count likes that have been "thumbed up" through the link on thier website, and excludes Facebook likes made outside of the IMDB links.

However, using only the IMDB's facebooks likes is simple and easy to implement.
